Physics!

I'm fond of C++11 and physical simulation: one of my bigger personal projects is a toy physics engine. Check it out on GitHub.


I encountered some specialized topics in this project, including a custom collision-contact algorithm and an implementation of constraint-based impulse physics. Please also ask me about these concepts: constraint islands, exponentially-decaying cameras and wind resistance, separating-axis theorem, Minkowski difference.


Academics

Previously, I studied computer science at UT Austin, completing the Turing Scholars honors program. In the absence of a game development track (which was rolled out in 2013), I took CS 378: Game Technology, the first game-development class offered at the school, as well as CS 379H: Honors Thesis, where I wrote a thesis about physical simulation of vegetation.

I am intimately familiar with the mechanics of a few games: Super Mario 64 (1996), Diablo 2: Lord of Destruction (2001), Super Smash Bros. Melee (2001), and Pokémon XY (2013). Aside from game development, I'm a piano player, gymnastics doer, and smoothie maker; I also do design work in my spare time.
