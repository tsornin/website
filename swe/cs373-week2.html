11 September 2011

No random writer this week. Maybe I could make a script to automate random writing everyone's blogs...

I'm actually enjoying the many different tools this class emphasizes. They are all very practical tools used in Real Life so I am interested in them beyond just résumé stuffing. For example, just today I read that the makers of Amnesia: The Dark Descent, Frictional Games, doesn't have an office and uses daily SVN commits to get things done.

Likewise, the issue tracker is useful. While working on Project 1, I was surprised how well it replaced my usual text-file todo-list.

I'm still confused about how the graders are verifying that programs work on Sphere, since Sphere usually wants programs in a special format. Do they use scripts to re-arrange submitted programs?

While I like the projects, I have mixed feelings about Sphere. While unhelpful error messages like "NZEC" and "Wrong Answer" may suitable for competition-style grading, they also promote "learning the hard way" and force students to blindly rewrite their programs — sometimes not because of logical errors, but just to cope with the SPOJ's many idiosyncrasies.

I also remain unconvinced that pair programming is the panacea for software development the authors of Extreme Programming Installed think it is. However, I do think it's very useful for tasks with many small deliverables — exactly what Project 2 asks for.
