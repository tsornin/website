CS 373: Week 1

4 September 2011

It was 10:00 PM on Sunday and I was going to write a blog post about my new favorite CS class, CS 373: "Software Engineering" with Professor Glenn P. Downing. These blog posts were assigned no doubt to assess student opinion of the class and get some useful feedback.

    At first, my head was filled with ideas about what I would blog about:
  • How I had already read several of the assigned readings on pair programming;
  • How the "hot seat" lecture questions encouraged (nay, forced) participation;
  • How afraid I was that git would eat my files;
  • How nervous I was when using a typeless language like Python;
  • How I could apply these new toolchains in Real Life.

I thought that only a few overachieving students would write blogs about a class. But I underestimated the allure of those ten extra credit points. As I procrastinated, and blog post after blog post popped up on Piazza, I realized that I was not a very beautiful or unique snowflake. Many of the things I wanted to say about the class had already been said, probably more elegantly than I could say them, by my classmates.

In fact, I figured that my blog would end up looking like a regurgitation of everyone else's thoughts. Fortunately, I had just the tool for such a job: Two years ago in Professor Calvin Lin's CS 315H class, I was assigned a "Random Writing" project: a program which chewed on input files and randomly produced text similar to the input files. The remainder of this blog post is composed of (almost) all the other CS 373 blogs out there, largely thanks to RandomWriter.java.
