Ray-traced stereoscopic image for CS 384G 2: Computer Graphics.

Is my scribble worth more than the paper it's scribbled on? Did I contribute positive value to this piece of paper — would I rather have this drawing, or the same unblemished sheet? Even better…would someone else?

I'm not being sarcastic here. There's definitely a bar for creating something better than the raw materials it's made from. It's pretty hard to make something good of a blank canvas — and we're not talking about the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel, or an empty unlined Moleskine notebook, or even intimidatingly fancy textured card-stock: we're talking an ordinary leaf of everyday notebook paper.
